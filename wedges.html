Short Game

Here comes the really hard part.

PUBLISHED SEPTEMBER 21, 2017.

Well now all the easy stuff is over. Yes, you heard that right, that was the easy stuff. The short game is the part of golf with the most diversity in the shots you'll have to hit. It's also the part of the game that the pros spend the most time working on, because it's where your ability to put up a good score really comes from.
Everyone swings their wedges a little different because the shorter shot allows for some more creativity in alignment and stance. This tutorial focuses on the most common swing type that allows for good accuracy and easy distance control.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder width apart or narrower if you feel comfortable.
  2. Line up the ball an inch or two back from the middle of your stance.
  3. Pull the club back keeping your left arm straight and right arm in towards your ribs.
  4. For a full swing, stop when your left arm is parallel to the ground. Essentially cutting off your swing just short of an iron.
  5. At the top of your swing your shoulders should be rotated 90 degrees while keeping your eye on the ball.
  6. At impact make sure to hit ball first, then ground. Just like your irons.
  7. Follow through and strike a pose.
  8. Then throw your club because you just shanked it and have to do this shit again.
