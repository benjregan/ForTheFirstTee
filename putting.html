Putting

Oh boy.

PUBLISHED SEPTEMBER 21, 2017.

Ok so we might have lied about the short game being the hardest part of golf. Many would argue that putting can literally drive you insane, but that won't happento you... At least, probably not.
Anyway putting is more about feel and your ability to read a green than the stroke itself. Although the stroke is very important because it needs to be easily repeatable. Like wedges, there are tons of ways to swing a putter, and it's up to you what grip, stance, and tempo are most comfortable. We'll teach you the basics, but unfortunatly you'll have to figure out the rest through practice.

Step by Step

  1. Examine the path your ball has to travel to get to the hole. This is called "reading the green."
  2. Determine how the slope of the green will affect where you have to aim. Get low to see the smaller hills easier.
  3. Your putter will have some alignment method on the back of it, that should be pointing where you want the ball to go.
  4. Bring the club straight back using nothing but your shoulders. Your arms should just swing like a pendulum.
  5. Accelerate the putter head through the ball with the pendulum-esque motion.
  6. Then throw your putter because you just lipped out and have to do this shit again.
